About Disaster

An Disaster is a sudden and violent shaking of the ground, caused by the movement of tectonic plates beneath the Earth's surface. These seismic events can range from minor tremors to devastating quakes that cause widespread destruction. Earthquakes are most commonly associated with fault lines, where stress accumulates until it is released as seismic waves. Modern technology, such as seismographs, helps scientists detect and measure earthquakes, improving preparedness and response strategies.
